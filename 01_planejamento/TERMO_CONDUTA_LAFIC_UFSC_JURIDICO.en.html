1. Purpose

This Term establishes, in a binding manner, the framework for ethical conduct, safety, institutional coexistence, and information protection applicable within the project scope.

2. Scope

All persons who participate, under any type of affiliation, in in-person or remote activities related to the project are subject to this instrument, including fieldwork, laboratory work, institutional communications, and digital media.

3. Principles

  • Legality, impartiality, morality, transparency, and efficiency, where applicable.
  • Scientific integrity, objective good faith, and technical responsibility.
  • Mutual respect, equity, and prohibition of discrimination.
  • Primacy of people’s safety and environmental protection.

4. Ethical conduct and coexistence

Any practice of harassment, discrimination, violence, coercion, intimidation, humiliation, or conduct incompatible with human dignity and the project’s academic-institutional purpose is expressly prohibited.

5. Safety and biosafety

Activities must comply with risk prevention protocols, access control procedures, biosafety safeguards, and measures aimed at preventing organism translocation and environmental damage.

6. Data protection, image, and sensitive information

The processing of personal data, images, records, and sensitive information shall observe defined purpose, necessity, proportionality, security, and consent, when required by law.

7. Public communication and intellectual property

The disclosure of project results, images, and information depends on institutional alignment when involving preliminary, strategic, or potentially institutionally or environmentally risky content.

8. Reporting channel

Situations of risk, irregularity, harassment, discrimination, or non-compliance may be formally reported to the Coordination, the Operational Lead, or UFSC’s competent institutional channels, ensuring procedural confidentiality and prohibition of retaliation against those reporting in good faith.

9. Consequences

Failure to comply with the provisions of this Term may result in warning, restriction of participation, dismissal, and other applicable administrative measures, according to severity and institutional regulations.

10. Final provisions

This instrument complements UFSC and LAFIC internal rules, without replacing them. Omitted cases shall be assessed by the Coordination, with support from the Operational Lead, in accordance with applicable law.
